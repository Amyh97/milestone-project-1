Profile - Amy Hollis

I have always been interested in IT and what goes on “behind the scenes” of an application. At school I was a digital champion and enjoyed IT at both GCSE and BTEC levels. In my current and previous job, I have had significant exposure to industry specific IT systems which has rekindled my interest in IT. My research into the IT world led me to web design and web development which caught my attention and is an area I wanted to investigate further. Having been sign posted to The Code Institute’s 5 Day Challenge, I really enjoyed the mini project and decided to take this lead further and qualify as a Full Stack Developer. I also like being able to see the finished product of my work, which this will allow me to do.

Once I had decided on web design/development it was clear that I was going to need to complete training before starting out in the industry, which led me to finding the Full Stack Development Diploma with Code Institute. I planned to train part-time alongside working full time. However, at the time of doing the Full Stack Development Diploma, I was put on temporary leave from work due to the COVID-19 outbreak. This meant that I was able to focus on this full time, allowing me to make best use of my time off and gain the skills required for the industry as quickly as I could.

On completion of the Diploma I would like to go into web-design and web-development. I want to create beautiful websites that are not only visually appealing, but have an excellent UX to help businesses achieve their goals by improving user loyalty by using intuitive designs that encourage users to explore the site. I wish to help a large variety of companies, small and large, to achieve their potential. I feel a good online presence and website are key to the success of a business, with the internet becoming a bigger part of our everyday lives.
